What Effects Your SAT scores?

This Visualization explores the relationship between racial demographics, test enrollment, math/english and the Average SAT Scores for NYC Public Schools

The charts below show the average SAT scores along with a relationship. Math and Reading scores are stongly positively corelated, as can be expected.

An interesting discovery that can be found is that schools that have a high number of test participation tend to have higher scores.

Another discovery that can be found is that schools with higher percentages of white and asian students tend to score higher on the SAT.

The magnitude of the negative correlations found in black and hispanic percentages are not as strong as the magnitude of the correlations between white and asian students
